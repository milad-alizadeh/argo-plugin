# Why argo designer sessions are slow and token-heavy — and how to fix it without weakening the gate

## 1. Bottom line

Three drivers account for nearly all of the designer/builder asymmetry, and none of them is the verification rigor the owner wants kept. **(a) Screenshots are pulled the expensive way on purpose-defeating paths** — every session called `get_screenshot` in cheap URL mode, then `curl`'d the PNG and `Read` it back into context as base64, re-incurring the exact image cost URL mode exists to avoid (fix-designer: 7 screenshot Reads at 48k–64k tokens each; builder-at-gate: 5 PNG reads ≈ 617k token-equivalent; cold-open: screenshots ≈ half the 213k budget). **(b) Nothing is ever pruned, so every large payload is re-paid on every subsequent turn** — fix-designer's `cache_read_input_tokens` climbs to 237,955 of a 240,586-token total, i.e. ~99% of the bill is re-serving context already seen, not new work. **(c) Rework spirals** on components that fight back — component-queue burned 78 of 138 Figma calls (56%) on one TreeNode connector saga, including its single largest payload (113.8k tokens). The audit gate itself is *not* the problem: it is load-bearing and delivers every tier-0 check. The waste is in *how payloads enter and persist in context*, not in *how much we verify*.

For scale: the four designer sessions cost **1,266,070 tokens across ~99 min for 4 deliverables** (~316k tok each); the reference builder shipped **two entire plans, 20 commits, 400 tests, in 198,280 tokens / 25 min**. A single hi-fi screen currently costs ~1.6x what a builder spends shipping two full plans.

## 3. Balanced recommendations

Every item below is **reliability-neutral or reliability-positive**. Ordered by leverage.

| Lever | Expected saving | Effort | Reliability impact |
|-------|-----------------|--------|--------------------|
| **R1. Ban `curl`+`Read` of screenshot URLs. Use `get_screenshot` base64 only when visual reasoning is genuinely needed, node-scoped, and treat the image as consumed-then-dropped (don't re-reference).** The correct split per Figma's own docs: base64 `get_screenshot` for the agent's own looking, `download_assets`/URL for final delivery — never round-trip a delivery URL back into context to look at it. | **Largest single win.** Removes the ~30–40% waste bucket; on fix-designer alone the 7 Reads were the whole token story. Est. 25–35% off a screenshot-heavy session. | Low — skill/agent-prompt wording + one lint on the transcript pattern | **Neutral.** Same pixels reach the agent; we just stop paying for them N more times. |
| **R2. Prune/collapse stale large tool results once acted on** — old screenshots and old `get_design_context` dumps get summarized to a one-line finding ("gap off 4px on node X, fixed") and the raw payload drops out. Add a compaction checkpoint at each component boundary. | Attacks the quadratic amplifier (#2); on a 20-loop session this is the difference between ~210k and ~20k cumulative billed input. Est. 20–30% on long sessions. | Medium — harness/agent behavior; partially a prompt discipline, ideally a hook | **Neutral-positive.** The *finding* is retained; only the redundant raw bytes leave. Gate evidence (receipts) is unaffected. |
| **R3. Route large one-off payloads through a read-isolated subagent** — bulk tier-0 audits, big get_design_context pulls, screenshot batches run in a fresh child context and return only a short summary + pass/fail to the parent. | Keeps the 500k-char audit/context dumps out of the parent's quadratic tail entirely. Compounds with R2. | Medium — new subagent wiring in orchestrate/design-screen | **Positive.** The deterministic gate still runs in full; the parent just doesn't carry its raw output. Isolation also reduces cross-contamination. |
| **R4. Drop R3(a) stroke-thickness + R3(b) bound-spacing numeric predicates from visual self-review.** ⚡ **Already enabled by today's deterministic tier-0.** NEW-3 stroke-scale-mismatch and D24 gap-padding are hard gates that fail loud on exactly these two defect classes *before* self-review runs; the SKILL text itself concedes R3(a) is "the visual confirmation, not a substitute." A bound gap's rendered value *is* the token value by Figma's binding mechanics once D24 is clean. | Removes an extra get_design_context/screenshot round trip **per component set**. Est. 5–10%. | Low — delete two sub-steps from designer.md + figma-create SKILL | **Positive.** Deterministic gate is stronger than an eyeball re-read (research: hard verifiers 113/120 vs LLM-judge 95/120). We keep R3(c) clipping/HUG — that one is *not* redundant. |
| **R5. Cache the tier-0 audit bundle** — reference it instead of re-pasting 18.5KB of function library into `code` on every run. | ~10–15k tok per extra audit run; cold-open would have saved ~30k. | Low–medium — kit change to load the bundle by ref | **Neutral.** Same audit code executes. |
| **R6. Checkpoint-and-report when a component fights back** (combineAsVariants corruption, ABSOLUTE-children, HUG fights) instead of push-through. When a fix loop exceeds N cycles on one node, stop, screenshot once, report state, move to other scoped work. | Caps the tail risk that produced 56% of component-queue in one saga. Highly variable but this is the single biggest *session-level blowup* mode. | Low — the EFFICIENCY cap already says "cap at 2 iterations absent a concrete defect"; enforce it as a hard stop, not guidance | **Neutral-positive.** A fought component that's silently wrong is worse than a reported one; this surfaces gate-adjacent tool bugs to a human earlier. |
| **R7. Enforce narrow reads**: `get_metadata` first, then `get_design_context`/`get_variable_defs` scoped to specific node IDs; never whole-frame. Restore batching toward the ~10-op ceiling. | Kills 537k-char single dumps; est. 5%. | Low — prompt discipline | **Neutral.** |

## 4. Do NOT do — tempting cuts that would cost reliability (rejected)

- **Do not cut or loosen the self-audit hard-gate loop** (create → audit → fix → re-audit). It's the biggest raw cost driver, but it is the *delivery mechanism for every tier-0 check*. Cutting it trades the whole gate for speed — rejected outright.
- **Do not drop cold-start live node-id re-verification.** It looks like a redundant round trip against a valid registry cache, but it guards a real build-time bug: `combineAsVariants` mints a *new* COMPONENT_SET id, invalidating cached ids. Tier-0 cannot substitute for this — different defect class. Keep.
- **Do not drop R3(c)** (no-clipping/misalignment + post-`combineAsVariants` HUG/width re-assertion). Unlike R3(a)/(b), no deterministic gate fully covers rendered-width-vs-content-width after a variant combine. Load-bearing.
- **Do not eliminate the final montage / visual self-review pass.** Downgrade its *numeric predicates* (R4), but keep the thin advisory look — it catches compositional/hierarchy problems a linter structurally cannot see. Just treat its output as signal, not proof (per `verify-designer-output`).
- **Do not remove the blind fidelity-verifier spawn to save the extra agent.** It's "partly-redundant-now" but it's the only *independent* compositional check; keep it as-is. Don't *expand* it, don't add more verifier spawns.
- **Do not reduce the audit frequency or batching-cap to hit a token target.** cold-open's final audit caught 6 real hard violations after the visual work "looked done" (5x missing-mode-copy, 1x non-semantic-binding). Fewer audits = shipped defects. The audits are cheap CLI round trips; the cost is the *payload persistence* (R2/R3/R5), not the checking.

## 5. Concrete minimal first move

**Eliminate the Read-of-downloaded-PNG pattern (R1) and nothing else, first.**

One change to `designer.md` / `figma-create` SKILL: screenshots for the agent's own visual reasoning use `get_screenshot` in base64 mode, **node-scoped**, only when a deterministic check can't answer the question — and the returned image is never re-referenced after the turn it's used. Forbid `curl`-ing a screenshot URL and `Read`-ing the PNG back into context. Add a transcript lint that flags `Read` of any `/tmp/*.png` that came from a Figma screenshot URL.

Why this one: it is the **single highest-leverage, lowest-risk, zero-reliability-cost** change. Every one of the four sessions independently names it as the most fixable inefficiency; it's the largest waste bucket (~30–40%); it requires no workflow restructuring, no gate change, no subagent wiring; and the agent still sees exactly the same pixels — it just stops paying for them on every subsequent turn. Ship R1, measure one designer session, then layer in R2 (pruning) and R4 (drop the now-redundant R3a/R3b predicates) as the fast follow-ups.
